Be grateful for MDMA

2011-12-07 — 3 min read
Sasha Shulgin is a legendary chemist and psychonaut and inventor of hundreds of psychedelics. In 1991, he and his wife, Ann, published a book. Part-novel and part-cookbook, PiHKAL—Phenethylamines I Have Known And Loved—told his story and the stories of the compounds he and his friends had ingested, a process he ironically termed ‘LAB’, or Large Animal Bioassay. It’s a gorgeous book, and a work that is still unmatched in its candour and novelty (and notoriety).
