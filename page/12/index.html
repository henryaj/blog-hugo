PRISM break

2014-01-30 — 4 min read
Most arguments sympathetic to mass surveillance programmes make two points: that they are reasonable (that is, have adequate regulatory oversight, only target valid intelligence targets, and are legal), and that they are effective. The first claim is demonstrably untrue. The second is doubtful, and unproven. — 1. Government mass surveillance programmes like PRISM and Tempora are illegal and out of control. We know that, for example, GCHQ lied to the UK National Security Council about the extent of their surveillance programme, that a US government privacy board believes NSA bulk collection of phone data is illegal1, that the NSA monitored calls of 35 world leaders and that they cracked the encryption used in the UN’s internal videoconferencing system (in clear breach of international law)2.
